Use JavaScript bookmarks for repetitive tasks at work

9-14-2013

It's useful if you need to frequently look up specific things, click the same buttons over and over, or anything else repetitive in the browser.

Go to settings and have your bar perminantly show before proceeding.

Try out a bookmark that does a simple alert by dragging this into your bookmarks bar: alert bookmarklet

You can select elements on the page with document.querySelectorAll(".class-name") and do whatever you want with them.

Basic Syntax

javascript: (() => { /* Your code goes here! */ })();

Example

Let's say you are working as Quality Assurance and need to fill out a form the same way multiple times a day when troubleshooting. Instead of having to type everything in, you can use a bookmarklet! You can have them saved on your desktop, so that when another repetitive task shows up, you can easily duplicate and modify your bookmarklet. It saves time, frustration, and errors.

It's not limited to just filling out forms, you can also change colors or delete elements to see information more easily.

Remember to test your bookmarklets!
